Abstract

Our project aims to provide affordable, solar-powered water filtration for rural communities. By utilizing locally sourced materials and a novel filtration membrane, we can reduce waterborne diseases by up to 40% in targeted villages. The system is modular, requiring minimal maintenance, and the solar component ensures it operates entirely off-grid.

quiz Questionnaire

Rural villages in our district lack consistent electricity, making standard filtration pumps useless. Our data shows 60% of illnesses in the local primary school are waterborne.
Our primary customers are local Panchayat boards and NGOs focused on rural health. Secondary markets include eco-resorts looking for sustainable water solutions.
Unlike existing RO systems that waste 3 liters of water for every 1 liter purified, our gravity-assisted membrane technology has near-zero wastage and requires no battery storage, reducing cost by 50%.
