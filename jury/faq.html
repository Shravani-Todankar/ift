Role & Responsibilities

What is my role as a Jury member in IFT?

expand_more
As a Jury member, you play a critical role in evaluating student innovations. Your responsibilities include reviewing assigned ideas, scoring them based on established criteria, providing constructive feedback, and helping identify the most promising innovations that will advance to the next round. Your expertise ensures fair and comprehensive evaluation of student submissions.

How are ideas assigned to me for evaluation?

expand_more
Ideas are automatically assigned to jury members through our intelligent allocation system. The system considers factors such as your expertise domain, workload balance, and availability. You will receive notifications via email and your jury dashboard when new ideas are assigned. Each jury member typically receives 15-25 submissions per round to ensure thorough evaluation.

Evaluation Process

What evaluation criteria should I use while scoring ideas?

expand_more
Evaluate each idea based on five key criteria: Innovation & Originality (uniqueness and creativity), Feasibility (practicality and resource requirements), Impact & Scalability (potential reach and social/economic benefit), Presentation Quality (clarity and professionalism), and Team Capability (skills and execution ability). Detailed rubrics with scoring guidelines are available in your evaluation dashboard.

What is weighted scoring and how does it affect evaluations?

expand_more
Weighted scoring means different evaluation criteria carry different importance in the final score. For example, Innovation & Originality might carry 30% weight, while Presentation Quality carries 15%. The system automatically calculates the weighted final score based on your individual criterion scores. This ensures that the most critical aspects of an innovation are given appropriate emphasis in the overall evaluation.

Submission Management

Can I edit or update my evaluation after submission?

expand_more
Yes, you can edit your evaluation before the round deadline. Navigate to "My Evaluations" in your dashboard, find the specific evaluation, and click "Edit". However, once the evaluation round closes, all scores are locked and cannot be modified to maintain integrity and fairness. Make sure to review your scores carefully before the deadline.

Where can I view my past evaluations and assigned ideas?

expand_more
All your evaluations and assigned ideas are accessible through your jury dashboard. Click on "My Evaluations" to view completed evaluations, or "Assigned Ideas" to see pending submissions. You can filter by round, status (pending/completed), and date. Each evaluation includes your scores, comments, and the idea details for future reference.

Shortlisting & Results

How does the shortlisting process work across rounds?

expand_more
The shortlisting process uses aggregated scores from all jury members who evaluated each idea. After the evaluation deadline, the system calculates average weighted scores for all submissions. Ideas are ranked, and the top performers (typically top 30-40% based on the round) advance to the next stage. The final shortlist is reviewed by the organizing committee before teams are notified. You can view shortlisting results in the "Round Results" section of your dashboard.

How long does each evaluation round last?

expand_more
Each evaluation round typically lasts 10-14 days from the date ideas are assigned. You'll receive clear deadlines via email and dashboard notifications. We recommend spreading your evaluations throughout the period rather than completing them all at the last moment to ensure thoughtful and quality assessments.
