Competition Overview

What is ENpower IFTx?

expand_more
ENpower IFTx is the school-level edition of the India Future Tycoon innovation competition. It empowers students from Grades 8–12 to form teams, brainstorm ideas, and submit innovative solutions to real-world problems. The competition is managed by your school's coordinator and evaluated by assigned jury members with AI-assisted scoring.

What are the different phases of the competition?

expand_more
The competition runs in four phases: Registration — students sign up and form teams; Idea Submission — teams submit their innovation ideas; Evaluation — AI and jury members score each submission on a 50-point scale; and Results — top-performing ideas are shortlisted and winners are announced. You can track progress across all phases from your dashboard.

How is the 50-point scoring system structured?

expand_more
Each submission is scored out of 50 points total — 25 points from AI evaluation (automated analysis of innovation, feasibility, and impact) and 25 points from Jury evaluation (expert human assessment of presentation, team capability, and real-world potential). The combined score determines the final ranking.

Your Role as Principal

What can I do on this platform?

expand_more
As the School Principal, your dashboard provides a read-only monitoring view of the entire competition. You can track student registrations, team formations, submission progress, evaluation scores, and final results — all in real time. You do not manage operations; that is handled by the School Administrator (coordinator).

Who manages the day-to-day competition operations?

expand_more
The School Administrator (competition coordinator) handles all operational tasks — onboarding students, managing jury members, setting deadlines, reviewing submissions, announcing results, and creating content. Your role is to oversee and monitor progress without needing to intervene in daily operations.

Can I export reports for school records?

expand_more
Yes. You can export data as CSV files from the Students, Teams, Submissions, and Results pages. The Reports page also offers a Print and Export PDF option for comprehensive analytics including grade-wise participation, score distribution, and top performers — ideal for school records, board meetings, or parent communications.

Students & Teams

How do students register and form teams?

expand_more
Students register through the ENpower IFTx student portal using their school credentials. Once registered, they can create a team (2–4 members) or join an existing one via team invitations. Each team selects a Team Leader who submits the final idea. You can monitor all registrations and team formations from the Students and Teams pages.

What does the participation rate indicate?

expand_more
The participation rate shows the percentage of registered students whose teams have submitted at least one idea. For example, a 76% participation rate means 76% of your students have actively contributed to an idea submission. This metric helps you gauge student engagement across different grades and sections.

Results & Evaluation

How are winners selected?

expand_more
Winners are selected based on their combined AI + Jury score out of 50. After all evaluations are complete, ideas are ranked by total score. The top 5 ideas (or as configured by the coordinator) are shortlisted as winners. You can view the full leaderboard, podium rankings, and shortlisted ideas on the Results page.

What happens after the school-level competition?

expand_more
Shortlisted ideas from the school-level IFTx competition may advance to the national-level India Future Tycoon competition, where they compete with innovations from schools across India. The Hall of Fame page showcases your school's top performers and their achievements for recognition and celebration.
